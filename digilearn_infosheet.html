About

Who we are and what we do

We are xCIT (xcit.org) a public cognitive sciences research group at the University of Luxembourg. Our goal is to better understand how the human mind works. More specifically, we’re interested in cognitive abilities (such as attention and learning), their interrelationships, how they vary with variables like age, gender or personality and how these abilities might be improved by training.

Participants in our studies will be asked to complete video-game-like computer tasks and fill out questionnaires on their computers.

If you decide to take part in this study, you will be asked to complete one or more tasks on your computer. It will typically take less than 1 hour to complete each task  and it might be required to complete a set of tasks in one sitting or distributed over multiple days (depending on the particular case). These tasks are either questionnaires (that is, text asking you various questions) or video game like tasks where you will be presented images and/or sounds and asked to perform some action in response to those (for example, pressing a key each time a particular digit is shown on the screen).  The questionnaires may ask you questions about your use of technologies, your motivation, your mood and your level of education; the video-game like task may ask you to make decisions about the locations of images on the screen, which shapes group together, or how different two sounds are. More details will be provided once enroll in a study.  

Our goal is to better understand the human mind in general, not to make any claims about any specific person.

Use by Children

Children are not allowed to participate in this study, you must be at least 18 years old to be allowed to participate in this study .

Risks and Benefits

There are minimal risks associated with this study. These include the potential to experience mild fatigue or boredom.  To our best knowledge, playing the games from this study should have no negative impact on your mental abilities.

There are also no direct benefits to participating in this study. While there is some research indicating positive mental impact of games, and although the games you’ll see in this study might look a lot like the games you’ve seen in the “brain training” category, we DO NOT claim that they improve cognition, delay dementia or even that they provide any scientifically validated diagnostic value.

By participating in this project, you will provide valuable data for science. We hope you will enjoy participating in this study.

Content

The games (computerized tasks) and questionnaires in our study are meant to be adequate for a large audience. If you disagree and would like to tell us why some specific content is problematic, please send us an email at contact@xcit.org   mentioning CONTENT as subject of the email.

In our opinion, the questions in the surveys are adequate for a wide audience. You are free to skip any question you do not want to answer, for example if they upset you or make you feel uncomfortable .

Voluntary Participation / Termination

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You are free not to participate or to withdraw at any time, for whatever reason and without having to justify yourself. Feel free to skip any of the questions you do not want to answer.

Note that it might be necessary for us to terminate a study earlier than expected. The two main reasons for us to do so are a) technical issues corrupting the study and b) participants showing clear signs of not taking the study seriously (e.g., pressing random keys).

Payment

Study participants will be financially compensated for their participation. The amount of this compensation is based on a fixed hourly rate and on the estimated duration of fully completed task (not the actual duration).

You will be paid for fully and seriously completed tasks. Taking breaks during a task will not affect the amount of your compensation. Rushing through the study on the other hand might compromise the quality of your data.

Rules of conduct

By consenting to participate in our study, you commit to taking the study seriously, dedicating your full attention to the tasks and responding accurately and honestly.

Data Privacy Policy

Principles

  • We will not sell any of your data to anyone;
  • We will not share your personal data (unless legally obliged);
  • We treat your data with confidentiality;
  • Only anonymized data may be shared openly.

Data Collection  

If you consent to participate in this study, we will collect data about your performance in our games and your responses and actions in the questionnaires.

Note that when you fill out the questionnaires we will record not only your answers (e.g., which option you clicked, which text you’ve entered); we will also record the timing of your actions (i.e., when did you click that option; when did you press which key). We only record your interactions within the games and questionnaires of this study. We do NOT record any data outside of these contexts (e.g., the text you might be typing for an email).

For the purpose of running this study we might need to store additional information such as device properties, software platform version, IP address, Media Access Control (“MAC”) address and unique device identifier or other device identifier (“UDID”), browser (e.g. “Chrome”), the date and time you accessed our online study.

Your data is pseudonymized. This means that the various data points that you will generate will be linked by an arbitrary code (e.g., bx#2245) rather than your actual name. All data is secured with industry-standard encryption, so that unauthorized parties can never read it. Only a handful of people at the University of Luxembourg working on this project are authorized to access that data. To our best knowledge, it is close to impossible for someone to get access to this data and identify you.

If you’re asked personal information or questions you don’t want to answer, please feel free not to. If you supplied personal information and later wish for it to be removed, please contact us at contact@xcit.org  mentioning PERSONAL as subject of the email.

Use of data

Data will be analyzed in order to generate insights about the human mind. The data analysis is executed by computer programs in the same way on all participants’ data to generate aggregate statistics.

We will not sell any data or use your data for purposes that are not directly related to scientific research.

If you have questions regarding the use of your data, please contact us at contact@xcit.org  mentioning USE OF DATA as subject of the email.

Tracking Technologies

Depending on whether you take part in our studies with a browser or a mobile app,  w e store either session cookies or application-specific cookie-like data on your device, for the sole purpose of allowing you not to re-enter your username and password every time you want to play. This data cannot be used to track you on the web like other cookies.  

Safeguarding and Retention of Personal Information.

We have implemented reasonable administrative, technical and physical measures in an effort to safeguard the personal information in our custody and control against theft, loss and unauthorized access, use, modification and disclosure. We restrict access to your personal information on a need-to-know basis to employees and authorized researchers who require access to fulfil their job requirements.

We have personal information retention processes designed to retain personal information for no longer than necessary for the purposes stated above or to otherwise meet legal requirements.

Access to Personal Information

You have the right to access, update and correct inaccuracies in your personal information in our custody and control, subject to certain exceptions prescribed by law. You may make a request to access, update and/or correct inaccuracies in your personal information we have in our custody or control. To do so, you must use the same channel that was used to provide the data. For example, if you log into a web service to participate in our study, you must use that same web service to request access or changes to your personal information ; this is to ensure that only you can access your data. If you have questions regarding how to access your personal data, please contact us at contact@xcit.org  mentioning PERSONAL as subject of the email.

Data sharing

In the spirit of open-science we ask you to agree to make your anonymised data public and freely accessible. Making data public is important because it allows other researchers to replicate our analyses and possibly challenge or confirm our conclusions. It may also allow other researchers to attempt interesting analyses we did not think of.

Importantly, we will never share your personal data, even if you agreed to share personal information with us. We will share data only to the extent that we believe it does not allow to identify or harm individuals.

Also, in the spirit of open-science, the results of our research that are based on your data will be freely accessible online (via pre-prints, open-access articles, or other forms). This way you can see for yourselves how and what your data is used for.
